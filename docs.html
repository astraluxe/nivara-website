No tech terms. Here's what the app actually is, why it runs on your laptop instead of the cloud, and who gets the most out of it.

What it actually is

Think about the tools you use for AI-related work today: maybe ChatGPT for writing, Zapier for automating, Copilot for code, a VPN or antivirus for safety. Each has its own login and bill, and they don't talk to each other. adris.tech is one desktop app that does all of it — and a few things that didn't have an affordable option before.

The biggest difference from any online AI tool: adris runs on your own machine. When you chat with an agent, that conversation isn't sent to a company's server. Your work, your files, your business conversations stay with you.

Who is this for?

You don't need to be technical. The people who get the most out of adris are:

  1. Freelancers working aloneKrew agents handle the time-consuming parts so you focus on the work only you can do.

Modules · 02

Studio — runs while you sleep.

A visual workflow builder: connect a trigger, drop in a Krew agent, connect an action — and it runs by itself, on a schedule. Like Zapier, but the workers are your AI agents and it runs on your laptop.

What it is

Studio is where you build automations — sequences that happen without you clicking anything. A workflow is three parts: something that starts it, an agent that does the thinking, and an action that delivers the result.

Real story — Freelance recruiter

Arjun does tech recruitment in Pune, 4–5 roles at a time.

Sorting each batch of CVs took 3–4 hours, several times a week. Now one Studio workflow runs at 9 AM: it reads new CVs, scores each candidate, drafts rejections, and makes Notion cards for the shortlist. He opens his laptop at 9:15 and it's already done.

Build it once. Watch it run.

An infinite canvas where every block is a step in your business

Drag a trigger from the sidebar, drop an agent in the middle, connect it to your tools on the right. That's a workflow — no code, no servers, no fragile glue.

One trigger fires three agents, pauses for your approval, then delivers to Notion, email and Slack.
  1. A trigger starts itA time, a new email, a form submission, a webhook — you choose what fires the workflow.
  2. An agent does the thinkingRead this, summarise that, decide, write a reply — the AI handles the judgement in the middle.
  3. An action delivers the resultSend an email, update Notion, write a Sheet, post to Slack — dropped where it belongs.

Start from a template

Pre-wired, pre-tested workflows you can plug your accounts into and run: auto-reply to leads, weekly client report, invoice tracker, social scheduler, support router, daily standup digest, and more.

ReplacesZapierMaken8n

Human in the loop

Pause the workflow at any step, get a WhatsApp or Slack notification, and approve before anything irreversible — a client email, a payment. On approve it ships; on reject it's discarded, with the reason logged. Every run is recorded with a full step-by-step trace.

Modules · 05

Vault — a private internet.

Blocks dangerous sites, ads and trackers before they reach your screen — for every app on your device, not just your browser. On by default, free with every account.

What it is

When you open a link, your device first looks up the address. Vault sits in that lookup and checks every request against lists of dangerous, ad-serving and tracking sites — blocking the bad ones before anything loads. Unlike a browser ad-blocker, it works system-wide.

Level 1Swift

Only known-dangerous sites. Nothing breaks.

DefaultBlock

Adds ads & trackers. Best for most people.

Level 3Guard

Stronger social-media tracking protection.

Level 4Core

Maximum blocking — for confidential work.

Level 5Family

Allow-list only. Good for a shared machine.

One switch, five levels — most people stay on Block.
Real story — Freelance designer

Mehul works from cafés in Ahmedabad — public Wi-Fi daily.

He clicked a link in a fake "client enquiry." Vault blocked the page instantly: "phishing — stopped before loading." In his first week it blocked 800+ tracker requests. Free, bundled, never configured.

Five protection levels

From "never break anything" to "lock it all down" — Swift, Block (recommended), Guard, Core and Family. Pick how aggressive it is; most people leave it on Block.

ReplacesNextDNSPi-holeBrowser ad blockers

Private by design

Every lookup is encrypted (DNS-over-HTTPS), so nobody on your network — not your ISP, not the café router — can log where you go. It checks every domain against malware and phishing lists too, so flagged domains never reach your device.

Modules · 07

Mesh — run models no single device can.

Links devices on the same Wi-Fi and shares their memory as one bigger machine — two 16 GB laptops become a 32 GB machine. No data leaves your network, nobody charges per use. Mesh 3 (3 devices) is free.

What it is

Better models need more memory than most laptops have. Mesh pools the RAM of devices on the same Wi-Fi, so the model runs across all of them — three 8 GB laptops become one 24 GB pool. It's live in the app today.

How it works

A large model is made of many layers. Mesh splits those layers across the devices in your group, so each only holds a slice — and they work together over the local network to run the whole model. That's why three small laptops can run something none could alone.

Real story — Student study group

Four engineering students in Coimbatore.

Their research needed a model too big for any one laptop; a paid service would have cost ₹40,000–₹80,000. They pooled four laptops on the college Wi-Fi into one 24 GB machine. A whole semester of experiments for ₹0.

Ways to use it

Pool your own devices (laptop + an old PC), a team's machines (idle memory chips in for heavy tasks), or students on the same Wi-Fi (hostel, library, study room). Up to 3 devices is always free; add more pay-as-you-go.
