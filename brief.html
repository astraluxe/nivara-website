The difference between adris.tech and a chat assistant is what happens after the answer. A chat assistant tells you how to find leads. adris.tech opens a browser, searches Maps and the web, collects the real companies, verifies the contact details, writes a different message for each one, opens the conversation on LinkedIn with the message ready to send, and remembers who replied — then does the follow-ups next Monday whether you're at the desk or not.

02

What it does for you

Everything below is one instruction typed in plain English. The time noted is what the same job costs a person doing it by hand.

Find your next 50 customers

≈ 6 hrs → 10 min
You say: "Find interior design studios in Pune who'd need our service, with founder names and contact details."

It runs live searches across Google Maps and the open web, pulls real businesses — never invented ones — and matches them to your size and city, so a solo founder gets reachable local firms rather than a list of unreachable enterprises. It then fills in the missing pieces: website, LinkedIn profile, phone, email.

The list is saved to your knowledge graph, so next month it builds on that list instead of starting over.

Run LinkedIn outreach without the grind

≈ 4 hrs → 15 min
You say: "Scan my LinkedIn connections, then write each of them a message about what we do."

It reads your actual connection list from your own logged-in browser, drafts a distinct message per person based on their role and background — not one template with a name swapped in — and then opens each person's chat window with the message copied and ready. You read it, you press send. Nothing goes out without you.

Replies and progress are tracked per contact, so a week later you can say "continue where we left off" and it picks up the campaign rather than restarting it.

Turn a request into a finished deck

≈ 5 hrs → 20 min
You say: "Make a 12-slide investor deck for us. Put our logo on every slide and use the product screenshots I attached."

You get a complete, designed presentation — structure, copy, visuals, your branding — built from what it already knows about your business. Generated imagery is available where you want it, and your own pictures always take priority over anything the AI makes.

Editing stays in the conversation: "make slide 4 about pricing", "use the darker blue", "drop the last slide". No re-export, no going back to a design tool.

Read the contract before you sign it

≈ 3 hrs or a legal bill → 5 min
You say: "Read this vendor agreement and tell me what I should be worried about."

It returns risky clauses, one-sided terms, missing protections and liabilities in plain language, with a risk score and section-by-section findings — enough to negotiate with, or to decide the document needs a lawyer's eye after all.

It handles scanned and photographed documents, files running to hundreds of pages, and Hindi and regional Indian languages. The document is read entirely on your own machine — it is never uploaded anywhere.

Walk into the meeting already briefed

≈ 90 min → 3 min
You say: "Brief me on this company and the person I'm meeting there tomorrow."

It runs live research and comes back with a structured picture: what the company does and sells, market position, funding and financial signals, leadership, recent news — and a separate read on the individual: their role, background and what they're likely to care about. Ready before your coffee is.

Clear the inbox and the content calendar

≈ 2 hrs a day → 20 min
You say: "Summarise everything unread, draft replies to the ones that need one, and write this week's posts."

Email triaged and answers drafted in your tone. Posts written for each platform in that platform's format, with character counts respected. Newsletters, blog posts, ad copy, captions and support replies come from named specialists who each do one thing well — and when you need options, you get several and pick one.

Have it happen without you

Recurring work → zero
You say: "Every Monday at 9, pull last week's numbers, write the client report and email it to them."

Describe the routine once and it becomes a live workflow. Triggers include a schedule, an incoming email, a new file in a folder, a payment, a calendar event, a GitHub event or a mention of you online. Results are delivered where your team already is — email, Slack, Notion, WhatsApp-length SMS, a spreadsheet, a document on your disk, or several at once.

On paid plans these keep running in the cloud when your computer is off — the report still lands on Monday if you're on a flight.

Build and ship the product too

≈ 30–50% off delivery time
You say: "Find the bug in this file, write the tests, and explain what you changed."

A full code editor and real terminal sit inside the same app, with an assistant that already sees the open file and what you just ran. Useful whether you have engineers or you're the founder holding the codebase together on weekends.

05

What's included

Eight modules, one install, one login. Every module is on every plan including Free — the plan governs how much you use, not what you can touch.

Krew — your AI team

48 specialists across sales, content, marketing, support, research, design, product and engineering, led by a chief of staff who assigns the work. You describe the outcome; the right people are put on it.

Instead of: five hires, or a chat AI you babysit

Automation — work that repeats itself

Turn any routine into a workflow that fires on a schedule or an event and delivers the result where your team already works. Describe it in a sentence, or draw it on a canvas.

Instead of: Zapier or Make, plus the person who maintains them

Guard — documents & safety

Contracts and agreements read and risk-flagged in plain language, including scanned files and Indian languages. Plus quiet monitoring of the machine itself.

Instead of: a legal first-pass bill on every document

Brain — one shared memory

Everything the agents find and produce is stored as a visible, linked knowledge graph — lead lists, research, drafts, files, per-contact progress. Work is never re-done or re-billed, and you can see and edit what the AI remembers.

Instead of: scattered notes and repeated research

Coder — build and fix things

A real editor and terminal with an assistant that has your code in context. Works with cloud models or entirely offline.

Instead of: a separate code-assistant seat

Models — AI that runs on your laptop

51 open models from tiny to frontier scale, filtered to what your machine can actually run. Free to use, offline, and never counted against your plan.

Instead of: per-token API bills for routine work

Vault — privacy across the machine

One switch blocks ads, trackers, malware and phishing for every browser and app at once — not just one browser with an extension.

Instead of: a per-device blocker subscription

Mesh — pool your computers

Combine the memory of several machines you already own to run models none of them could run alone.

Instead of: renting GPU time

07

Your work stays yours

adris.tech is a desktop application, not a website. Your conversations, contracts, research and credentials sit on your own machine — which is the point, not a footnote.

WhatWhere it lives
Conversations with your agentsOn your machine. Never synced to us, never used to train anything.
Contracts and documents you scanRead on your machine. They are never uploaded.
Passwords, tokens and API keysIn an encrypted store on your machine. Calls go from your computer straight to the service.
Your knowledge graph and filesOn your machine.
Your account and planWith us — email, plan and usage counts only. Hosted in Mumbai.

If you want nothing at all to leave: connect your own AI key — free tiers are available and it takes two minutes — or run one of the local models. Requests then go from your machine directly to the provider, or nowhere at all. Either way your usage becomes unlimited from our side, on every plan including Free.

Practical safeguards, too: agents treat anything they read from the web or an inbox as untrusted, so a malicious page can't give them instructions; terminal commands need your approval; and when an agent is driving a browser window, it says so on screen.

09

Judge it in 30 minutes

The Free plan is a real evaluation, not a teaser. No card, no sales call, no demo booking required. Install it and give it something that actually matters this week.

  1. Install from adris.tech/download and sign in — about two minutes, roughly a 10 MB download.
  2. Ask it to find twenty real prospects in your city and check the names against reality. This is the fastest test of whether it invents things.
  3. Drop in a contract you've already read, and see whether it flags what you flagged.
  4. Ask for a deck or a week of content and judge the first draft on its merits.
  5. Set one recurring job — a Monday report, a daily digest — and let it run without you.

Stated plainly: adris.tech runs on Windows today. macOS and Linux builds are coming, and there is no mobile app or browser version. If your evaluation depends on those, it's worth waiting — we'd rather say so here than after you've spent the afternoon.
