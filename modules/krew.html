02How agents work

They don't guess.
They reason and verify.

Every agent runs a ReAct loop — Reason, Act, Observe, repeat — before giving you an answer. They search the web, read your connected tools, verify what they find, and only then reply. No hallucinating, no shortcuts.

  • Up to 10 reasoning steps per task
  • Web search, file reads, API calls — all in-loop
  • Sees tool output before continuing — not guessing
  • Stops and asks if it genuinely can't proceed
1
User
"Write a LinkedIn post about our v1.0 launch — make it feel like a milestone, not a press release."
2
Zara.Content · Reason
I need context on what v1.0 actually includes before I write. Let me search for recent product-launch post formats that feel authentic, then ask what shipped.
3
Act
Calling web search for best practices.
web_search("authentic product launch LinkedIn post 2024")
4
Observe
Result: 3 high-performing frameworks — story arc (struggle → solution → milestone), team shoutout format, and behind-the-scenes format. Story arc has 4× the average engagement.
5
Zara.Content · Final answer
Drafting with story arc: opening hook about the day you started, 3 milestones, team acknowledgement, and a close that invites the reader in. Tone: human, not corporate.
03Built to think

Not a chatbot.
An actual agent.

Four systems run under the hood on every task — invisible to you, but the reason Krew's answers feel different from a plain chat window. These aren't prompts. They're mechanics baked into the loop.

Memory
Remembers you across sessions

Each agent can save facts with save_memory and recall them in future conversations. Tell Zara your brand voice once — she uses it every time. Tell Kunal which metrics matter — he tracks them without being reminded. Memory is per-agent and stored locally on your machine.

Planning
Plans before it acts

For any multi-step task, every agent writes a numbered plan before touching a single tool. You see the plan first. You can redirect before a single API call is made. No blind tool-calling, no surprises halfway through a 10-step workflow. Plan → execute → done.

Self-reflection
Reviews its own answer

After drafting a response, the agent runs a second pass — asking itself whether the answer is complete, accurate, and worth sending. If not, it revises before you see it. What arrives in your chat is the second draft, not the first. The mistakes get caught before they reach you.

Boss delegation
Arjun.Boss assigns the right agent

Give Boss a complex task and he breaks it into subtasks, assigns each to the right specialist, coordinates the results, and returns one unified answer. Zara handles the copy. Neo handles the code review. Ava handles the roadmap. You talk to one agent, your whole office works.

06Privacy · local-first

Your Krew.
Your device. Your data.

Krew runs entirely on your laptop. Conversations are stored in a local SQLite database. Your credentials are encrypted in your OS keychain. We never see your chats, your connected tools, or what your agents do. Zero cloud exposure — by architecture, not by policy.

Conversations stay local

Every chat, every tool call, every agent response — stored in a SQLite file on your machine. Nothing leaves unless you explicitly export. No cloud sync, no background upload. Delete the file and the data is permanently gone — no recovery from any server because it was never there.

Credentials in your keychain

Gmail tokens, Notion secrets, GitHub PATs — encrypted in your OS keychain (Windows Credential Manager) and never written to disk in plain text. Krew reads them at runtime only. adris.tech's servers never see them, not even during setup.

Bring your own AI key

Connect your Gemini, OpenAI, or Groq key and requests go directly from your laptop to the AI provider — adris.tech never sees the prompt or the response. Your conversations are between you and the model. We have no way to read them even if we wanted to.
