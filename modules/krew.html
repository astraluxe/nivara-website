00 What is Krew

An AI team that does the work — not just answers questions.

48 specialists · 10 departments · all yours on every plan · runs locally

When most people think "AI", they think of a chat window: you type a question, it types back, and you copy-paste the answer somewhere. Useful, but you're still doing the real work — turning that text into a finished email, a posted caption, a reviewed pull request.

Krew flips that. Instead of one general chatbot, you get a team of named specialists — Arjun the chief of staff, Zara the caption writer, Neo the code reviewer, and 40 more. You give one a task in normal language, and it goes and does it: searches the web, reads your files, drafts the email, and brings back something finished. You're reviewing, not doing.

A normal AI chatbot
  • Answers, then stops
  • Forgets you between chats
  • Can't open your tools or the web
  • You still do the actual work
  • Your words go to a company server
A Krew agent
  • Finishes the task, end to end
  • Remembers your work permanently
  • Reads Gmail, Notion, GitHub + the live web
  • Hands you a ready result
  • Runs on your laptop — nothing uploaded

01 Your AI office

Each one has a name. Each name means a job.

Name.Role — like Zara.Content or Neo.Engineer — so you always know who's doing what

Krew isn't one chatbot you rename. It's 48 distinct specialists, each trained for a single job and shown in chat as Name.Role. Arjun.Boss runs the show and hands tasks to the right person. Zara.Content writes your captions. Vera.Engineer reviews your code. Nyx.Data digs up companies from public databases. All 48 are included on every plan — nothing is locked behind a higher tier.

Real story — Content agency owner

Priya runs a small content agency in Hyderabad — 7 clients, one employee.

Every week she writes LinkedIn posts for all 7 clients — 3 each, every one a different tone and industry. That's 21 posts, plus monthly reports and the emails that go with them. It used to eat roughly 2 full working days a week.

Now she has a Krew agent set up per client, each one taught that client's brand once. When she needs this week's posts she types: "write this week's 3 posts for Neeraj's SaaS company." Three ready-to-post captions come back in about 90 seconds. Two days of work is now about three hours of her attention.

02 How it thinks

It doesn't guess. It reasons, then verifies.

Reason → Act → Observe → repeat — up to 10 steps before it answers

A plain chatbot answers from memory, which is why it sometimes confidently makes things up. Every Krew agent works differently: it thinks about what it needs, uses a tool to go get it, looks at what came back, and only then continues. It's the difference between a confident guess and a checked answer.

Here's that loop on a real task — writing a launch post — shown exactly as it runs inside the app:

1
You
"Write a LinkedIn post about our v1.0 launch — make it feel like a milestone, not a press release."
2
Zara.Content · Reason
I need to know what makes a launch post feel authentic before I write. Let me look up high-performing formats first.
3
Act
Searching the web for current best practices.
web_search("authentic product launch LinkedIn post 2024")
4
Observe
Found 3 frameworks — story arc, team shoutout, behind-the-scenes. The story arc gets 4× the average engagement.
5
Zara.Content · Final answer
Drafting with the story arc: a human opening hook, three milestones, a team thank-you, and a close that invites the reader in. Tone: human, not corporate.
You can watch each step happen — so you always know why the answer is what it is.
  1. Up to 10 reasoning steps per taskComplex jobs get multiple rounds of think-act-check, not one rushed reply.
  2. Real tools, in the loopWeb search, file reads, and app calls happen mid-task — the agent sees real results before continuing.
  3. It stops and asks if it's genuinely stuckNo quiet guessing. If it can't proceed safely, it tells you instead of inventing an answer.
03 Built to think

Not a chatbot. An actual agent.

Four systems run on every task — the reason Krew feels different from a chat window

These aren't clever prompts you have to write. They're built into how every agent works, quietly, on every task you give it.

Memory
Remembers you across sessions

Tell Zara your brand voice once — she uses it every time. Tell Kunal which metrics matter — he tracks them without being reminded. Memory is per-agent and stored on your machine.

Planning
Plans before it acts

For any multi-step task, the agent writes a numbered plan before touching a single tool. You see the plan first and can redirect before any action is taken.

Self-review
Checks its own answer

After a first draft, the agent runs a second pass — is this complete, accurate, worth sending? What arrives in your chat is the second draft, not the first.

Delegation
Boss assigns the right agent

Give Arjun.Boss a big task and he splits it up, hands each piece to the right specialist, and returns one unified answer. You talk to one agent; your whole office works.

Memory, planning, self-review and delegation — working together on every request.

07 Privacy · local-first

Your Krew. Your device. Your data.

Conversations on your disk · credentials in your OS keychain · zero cloud by design

This is the part that's genuinely different from any online AI tool. Krew runs on your laptop. Your chats live in a database file on your machine, your passwords are encrypted in the operating system's keychain, and we never see any of it. Not because we promise not to look — because there's nothing on our side to look at.

Conversations stay local

Every chat and tool call is stored in a SQLite file on your machine. Nothing leaves unless you export it. Delete the file and it's gone — there's no server copy, because there never was one.

Credentials in your keychain

Gmail tokens, Notion secrets, GitHub keys — encrypted in your OS keychain, never written to disk in plain text. adris.tech's servers never see them, not even during setup.

Bring your own AI key

Connect your own Gemini, OpenAI or Groq key and requests go straight from your laptop to the provider — we never see the prompt or the response.
