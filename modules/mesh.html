00 What is Mesh

Small devices, enormous models.

Share memory across devices on the same Wi-Fi · run bigger AI · pay nothing per use

Better AI models need more memory than most laptops have — so most people are stuck running weaker models locally, or paying every time they use a powerful one online. Mesh connects devices on the same Wi-Fi and pools their memory as one bigger machine. The model runs across all of them; no data leaves your network, and nobody charges you per use.

Real story — Student study group

Four engineering students in Coimbatore are working on an AI research project together.

Their research needed a powerful model — far more accurate than anything their individual laptops could run. A paid AI service for hundreds of experiments would have cost ₹40,000–₹80,000. Money none of them had.

They formed a Mesh group on the college Wi-Fi. With one click, the memory of all four laptops became one shared pool, and the model they needed loaded across all four. They ran a whole semester of experiments for ₹0 — and finished two weeks ahead.

01 How it works

One pool, split across machines.

The model's layers are spread over the devices — each one holds a slice

A large model is made of many layers. Mesh splits those layers across the devices in your group, so each machine only has to hold a slice in memory. They work together over the local network to run the model end to end — which is why three small laptops can run something none of them could alone.

  1. Devices join a roomEveryone on the same Wi-Fi joins a Mesh room with one click — no setup, no accounts to wire up.
  2. Memory becomes one poolTheir RAM is combined into a single pool — three 8 GB laptops show up as 24 GB.
  3. The model is split by layeradris.tech routes different layers of the model to different devices automatically.
  4. It runs locallyThe whole thing runs on your own network — nothing is sent to a cloud, nobody bills you per use.
02 Pricing

Three devices. Always free.

Mesh 3 is free forever · larger or longer pools are pay-as-you-go

Up to three devices in a Mesh is free, with no expiry. That's enough for most study groups and small teams. If you need a bigger pool or a longer session, you can add devices and pay only for what you use — pick the size, pick the duration.

The bottom line: if you've ever wanted a better AI model but found it too expensive or too slow on your own laptop, Mesh solves it — using hardware you already own, shared with people you trust.
